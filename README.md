##  Project Overview

This project performs an end-to-end **Market Basket Analysis** on a
Kaggle groceries dataset. It covers the full data pipeline — from
loading raw data with missing values, cleaning it, counting item
frequencies, and visualizing the top 10 most purchased grocery items
through a styled bar chart. The project concludes with two
data-driven business insights grounded in retail strategy.

## 📊 Key Insights

**Insight 1 — Whole Milk is the Grocery Champion**
Whole milk is the single most purchased item, appearing in the
majority of transactions — outselling the second-ranked item by a
wide margin. Combining it with yogurt, dairy products alone account
for a dominant share of all purchases, making them the backbone of
the grocery basket.

**Insight 2 — Four Product Pillars Drive the Entire Basket**
The top 10 items reveal a clear Pareto-style pattern: a small number
of products across four categories (Dairy, Vegetables, Bakery,
Beverages) generate almost all transaction volume. This has direct
implications for inventory prioritization, cross-category bundling,
and shelf placement strategy.
